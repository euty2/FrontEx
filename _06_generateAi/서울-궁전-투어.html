Step back in time and discover the architectural marvels that housed Korea's royal families for centuries

Gyeongbokgung

경복궁

The largest and most magnificent of the Five Grand Palaces, Gyeongbokgung served as the main royal palace of the Joseon dynasty, featuring the iconic Gwanghwamun Gate and stunning mountain backdrop.

Changdeokgung

창덕궁

A UNESCO World Heritage site, Changdeokgung is renowned for its beautiful garden complex, Huwon (Secret Garden), and its harmonious integration with the natural landscape.

Deoksugung

덕수궁

Known as the "Palace of Virtuous Longevity," Deoksugung features a unique blend of traditional Korean and Western architecture, with a beautiful stone-wall road that's especially romantic in the evening.

Changgyeonggung

창경궁

The "Palace of Flourishing Gladness" is known for its beautiful gardens and ponds. Originally built as a residence for queens and concubines, it offers a more serene atmosphere than other palaces.
